# Sample Character: Call of Cthulhu 7e

> Example output generated by `rpg-lore-weaver` for a Call of Cthulhu 7e campaign set in 1920s Arkham.

```


════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  MARGARET "MAGGIE" CALLOWAY — The Archivist
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

 📌 ELEVATOR PITCH
   A widowed university librarian who discovered her
   late husband's hidden correspondence with a
   Miskatonic professor about "doors that open from
   the other side" — and needs to know if the thing
   she heard breathing in the attic was grief or
   something else entirely.

 👤 APPEARANCE & VIBE
   Mid-forties, sharp-featured, perpetually wearing
   reading glasses perched on her forehead. Ink-
   stained fingers. Keeps her auburn hair in a tight
   bun that loosens as the day (and her composure)
   unravels. Smells faintly of old paper and Earl
   Grey. Speaks in complete, precise sentences — a
   habit from years of correcting students. Hands
   tremble when she's tired, which she attributes
   to too much coffee.

 📖 BACKSTORY
   Margaret married Thomas Calloway at twenty-two —
   a union of genuine affection between a librarian
   and a civil engineer. They lived quietly in
   Arkham for eighteen years. Thomas died in 1921,
   officially of pneumonia. The doctor said it was
   unremarkable.

   Six months later, organizing Thomas's study,
   Margaret found a locked drawer. Inside: three
   years of letters between Thomas and Professor
   Edwin Marsh of Miskatonic's Restricted Collection.
   The letters discussed "acoustic phenomena in
   pre-Colonial structures" and referenced a site
   Thomas had surveyed in Dunwich — a foundation
   that "predated any known settlement by centuries."

   The final letter, dated two weeks before Thomas
   died, read: "I heard it again last night. Not
   from below this time. From the attic. Margaret
   was asleep. I checked — nothing there. But the
   dust on the floor showed impressions, Edwin.
   Something with too many limbs had been standing
   where I sleep."

   Margaret read that letter three times, locked it
   back in the drawer, and didn't sleep for a week.
   Then she heard it too — a slow, rhythmic sound
   from the attic, like breathing, like something
   patiently waiting. She told herself it was the
   wind. She doesn't believe that anymore.

 🧠 PSYCHOLOGICAL PROFILE
  • Personality: Methodical, observant, quietly
    stubborn. Will not leave a question unanswered
    even when every instinct says to walk away.
    Catalogs everything — including her fears.
  • Ideals: "If something can be known, it should
    be known. Ignorance is never safety — it is
    delayed catastrophe."
  • Bonds: Thomas Calloway (deceased husband) — she
    loved him, and now realizes she never truly
    knew him. The Restricted Collection at Miskatonic
    Library — she has a key. Thomas's final letter,
    folded and carried in her coat pocket.
  • Flaws: Insomnia since finding the letters.
    Cannot let a mystery remain unsolved, even when
    self-preservation demands it.
  • Fear: Claustrophobia — enclosed spaces above
    her (attics, lofts, low ceilings). The breathing
    from the attic. Once. Maybe twice.
  • Desire vs Need: WANTS to understand what Thomas
    found — to finish his work. NEEDS to accept
    that some questions destroy the asker.

 📋 CoC BACKSTORY ENTRIES
  1. Personal Description: A composed, bookish
     woman who looks ten years younger in good
     light and ten years older when she's afraid.
  2. Ideology: Knowledge is the only honest defense
     against the unknown.
  3. Significant Person: Thomas Calloway (deceased
     husband) — she loved him, and now she realizes
     she never truly knew him.
  4. Meaningful Location: The Restricted Collection
     at Miskatonic Library — she has a key.
  5. Treasured Possession: Thomas's final letter,
     folded and carried in her coat pocket.
  6. Traits: Methodical (she catalogs everything,
     including her fears).
  7. Injuries & Scars: Insomnia since finding the
     letters. Circles under her eyes that cosmetics
     can't hide.
  8. Phobias: Claustrophobia — enclosed spaces
     above her (attics, lofts, low ceilings).
  9. Arcane Tomes: None yet — but Thomas's letters
     reference "Marsh's private translation" of
     something he only calls "the Codex."
 10. Encounters: The breathing from the attic.
     Once. Maybe twice. She's not sure the second
     time wasn't a dream.

 🔗 CONNECTIONS
  • Ally: Helen Marsh — Professor Marsh's daughter,
    also a Miskatonic librarian. They share a desk.
    Helen doesn't know about the letters. Yet.
  • Mentor: Dr. Simon Orne — retired professor of
    folklore who once told her: "The old stories
    aren't cautionary tales. They're field reports."
  • Rival: Dean Whitmore — head of Miskatonic
    Library administration, who blocked Thomas's
    posthumous research grant. He knows more than
    he's saying.

 🎣 DM HOOKS (Keeper Hooks)
  • Secret: Thomas didn't die of pneumonia. His
    autopsy (sealed by Miskatonic) shows cause of
    death as "systemic organ restructuring" — his
    insides were rearranged, still functional, into
    a pattern no anatomy textbook recognizes.
  • Unresolved Thread: "The Codex" Marsh translated
    is a fragment of the Pnakotic Manuscripts. It
    describes how to close a door — but only from
    the other side.
  • Evolution Arc: Margaret must decide whether
    knowing the truth about Thomas is worth losing
    her own grip on reality — sanity as currency.
  • Cultural Touchstone: The Restricted Collection
    key she carries unlocks a reading room where
    Miskatonic scholars have gathered forbidden
    knowledge for decades — a living symbol of
    "knowledge at any cost."
  • Anchor: Thomas's letter (Backstory Entry #5).
    As long as she carries it, it costs 0 SAN to
    recall his face. If she loses it, the memories
    start to blur.

 ⚔️ MECHANICAL SUGGESTIONS (CoC 7e)
  • Occupation: Librarian
  • Key Characteristics: INT 75, EDU 80, POW 55
  • Credit Rating: 30 (modest but respected)
  • Skills: Library Use 75, History 60, Spot Hidden
    55, Listen 50, Persuade 45, Occult 25 (self-
    taught from Thomas's letters), First Aid 30
  • Luck: 40 (below average — dramatic tension)
  • Starting Sanity: 55 (POW × 5, minus 1d6 from
    the attic incident she hasn't reported)
  • Backstory Anchor: Entry #3 (Thomas Calloway)
```
