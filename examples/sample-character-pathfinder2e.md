# Sample Character: Pathfinder 2e

> Example output generated by `rpg-lore-weaver` for a Pathfinder 2e campaign in Golarion.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  SERAPHINE DUSKWALKER — The Oath That Burned
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

 📌 ELEVATOR PITCH
   A human Champion of Sarenrae who broke her own
   anathema to save a child, and now walks a path
   of self-imposed penance, unsure if her goddess
   has abandoned her or if the silence IS the test.

 👤 APPEARANCE & VIBE
   Tall, sun-weathered woman in her early thirties
   with close-cropped silver hair that was black
   before The Night. Burn scars climb her left
   forearm — self-inflicted in a purification rite
   that didn't work. Carries a scimitar with a
   cracked sunburst on the pommel. Moves with
   military discipline but pauses before doorways,
   as if asking permission to enter. Voice steady
   and low, breaks only when children laugh.

 📖 BACKSTORY
   Seraphine grew up in Absalom's Ascendant Court,
   daughter of a minor noble and a temple healer.
   She was ordained as a Champion of Sarenrae at
   eighteen — the youngest in her chapter. For ten
   years she was exemplary: healing the sick, burning
   the undead, bringing dawn where there was shadow.

   Then came Ravengro. A cult of Urgathoa had taken
   a village. Among the prisoners was a girl, maybe
   seven, shackled to an altar rigged with explosive
   runes. The cult leader offered a deal: let the
   ritual complete — only one soul consumed — and
   the other nineteen prisoners walk free.

   Sarenrae's edicts are clear: never let an evil
   act proceed uncontested. Seraphine's anathema
   forbids allowing harm through inaction AND through
   cooperation with evil. The trap was perfect —
   every choice violated something.

   She chose the nineteen. She held the girl's hand
   and lied: "Close your eyes, it will be over
   soon." The cult completed their rite. The girl
   died. The nineteen walked out.

   Seraphine's divine powers flickered that night —
   not gone, but diminished, unreliable. She hasn't
   heard Sarenrae's voice since. She doesn't know
   if that's punishment or mercy.

 🧠 PSYCHOLOGICAL PROFILE
  • Personality: Controlled, quietly intense,
    compulsively honest (since The Lie). Volunteers
    for first watch every night. Cannot abide broken
    promises — even trivial ones.
  • Edicts: Protect those who cannot protect
    themselves. Speak truth, especially when it hurts.
    Stand between the fire and the innocent.
  • Anathema: Never lie to a child. Never make a
    deal with evil again, regardless of the math.
    Never claim certainty about the divine.
  • Ideals: Redemption is real but must be earned
    through action, not prayer. No one is beyond
    saving — except possibly herself.
  • Bonds: The cracked scimitar (a gift from her
    ordination), a charcoal sketch of the girl she
    couldn't save (drawn from memory), a letter from
    her mother she hasn't opened in two years.
  • Flaws: Cannot make utilitarian choices anymore.
    Will sacrifice tactical advantage to save one
    person, even when the calculus says otherwise.
  • Fear: That she made the right choice in Ravengro
    — and that "right" choices can still damn you.
  • Desire vs Need: WANTS Sarenrae's voice back —
    divine confirmation that she is forgiven. NEEDS
    to forgive herself without permission from above.

 🔗 CONNECTIONS
  • Ally: Brother Castiel — a cleric of Pharasma
    who was in Ravengro. He doesn't judge her. He
    judges himself for arriving too late.
  • Mentor: High Sunlord Valeria (retired). Taught
    Seraphine that "faith is what you do when no one
    is watching, including your god." Refuses to
    discuss The Night.
  • Rival: Justicar Harren — a Champion of Iomedae
    who believes Seraphine's "crisis of faith" is
    actually cowardice. He's not entirely wrong.

 🎣 DM HOOKS
  • Secret: The girl in Ravengro had Urgathoa's
    mark on her palm. The cult didn't choose her
    randomly — she was already claimed. Seraphine
    doesn't know this.
  • Unresolved Thread: Her mother's unopened letter
    contains a confession — her mother knew about
    the cult months before Ravengro and chose not
    to report it, to protect a political ally.
  • Evolution Arc: Seraphine must learn that silence
    from the divine is not absence — Sarenrae's
    test is whether she can be good without reward.
  • Cultural Touchstone: In the Ascendant Court,
    new Champions still swear their oath on
    Seraphine's original scimitar (the uncracked
    one she left behind).

 ⚔️ MECHANICAL SUGGESTIONS (PF2e)
  • Class: Champion (Redeemer) of Sarenrae
  • Ancestry: Human (Versatile Heritage — Skilled)
  • Background: Martial Disciple (Lore: Theology)
  • Key Boosts: STR, CHA (faith), WIS (vigilance)
  • Edicts: Protect innocents, speak truth, stand
    between danger and the helpless
  • Anathema: Lie to a child, bargain with evil,
    claim divine certainty
  • 1st-Level Feat: Deity's Domain (Fire) — the
    flame that no longer answers reliably
