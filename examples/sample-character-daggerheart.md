# Sample Character: Daggerheart

> Example output generated by `rpg-lore-weaver` for a Daggerheart campaign with a narrative-focused tone.

```


════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  REN ASHVEIL — The Mender Who Breaks
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

 📌 ELEVATOR PITCH
   A faun healer who absorbs the pain of those they
   cure  and secretly craves the suffering because it
   makes them feel alive in a world that left them numb.

 👤 APPEARANCE & VIBE
   Tall for a faun, with bark-dark skin that cracks
   along the forearms in patterns that shift with mood.
   Antlers wound with dried wildflowers that they never
   replace  they prefer the beauty of decay. Warm
   voice, careful hands, eyes that linger a beat too
   long. Hums tunelessly when healing. Wears a satchel
   of herbs so heavy it leaves a permanent bruise on
   their shoulder. Smells of lavender and something
   sharper underneath  iron, maybe. Or grief.

 📖 BACKSTORY
   Ren was born in the Verdant Hollows, a community of
   faun healers who believed that pain is a river  you
   can redirect it, but never destroy it. Their mother,
   Elder Savia, was the greatest mender the Hollows had
   seen in three generations. She taught Ren that
   healing is not kindness  it's a transaction. "You
   take their pain. You carry it. That is the cost."

   Ren was good at carrying. Too good. By their teens,
   they had mended broken bones, fever-dreams, and a
   neighbour's grief after losing a child. But with
   each healing, Ren felt less. Colors dimmed. Food
   lost taste. Laughter became mechanical. The pain
   they absorbed didn't pass through  it stayed,
   layering like sediment, until Ren couldn't tell
   their own feelings from the borrowed ones.

   The breaking point came when their younger sibling,
   Lira, was struck by a corruption blight. Ren poured
   everything into the healing  and succeeded. But the
   cost was total: Ren lost the ability to feel joy.
   They can manufacture a smile, but the warmth behind
   it died on the floor of Lira's sickroom.

   Worse: in the hollow space where joy used to live,
   Ren discovered that absorbing pain now felt like
   something. The only thing. A sharp, electric aliveness
   that nothing else provided. They hate themselves for
   it. They can't stop.

   They left the Hollows the next morning without
   saying goodbye.

 🧠 PSYCHOLOGICAL PROFILE
  • Personality: Outwardly gentle, attentive, and
    selfless. Inwardly hollow and addicted to the only
    sensation they have left. Speaks with practiced
    warmth. Avoids silence because it forces them to
    notice the emptiness.
  • Ideals: Every living thing deserves to be free from
    pain  even if someone else must carry it. Healing
    is sacred. But Ren is starting to question whether
    the cost is too high.
  • Bonds: Lira (sibling, alive but estranged), the
    memory of Elder Savia's hands, a pressed flower
    from the Hollows they keep in their journal.
  • Flaws: Addiction to absorbing pain masquerading as
    selfless healing. Cannot refuse to help someone,
    even enemies. Lies about their own wellbeing
    instinctively.
  • Fear: That they will become someone who seeks out
    suffering in others  not to heal it, but to feel
    something. That the line between healer and
    predator is thinner than they think.
  • Desire vs Need: WANTS to find a way to feel joy
    again without the pain. NEEDS to accept the loss
    and find a new reason to exist  one that doesn't
    require suffering.

 🔗 CONNECTIONS
  • Ally: Dusk  A wandering bard who doesn't ask
    questions and fills silences with music. The only
    person around whom Ren doesn't feel the need to
    perform normalcy.
  • Mentor: Elder Savia (mother)  Taught them that
    pain is a river. Ren now wonders if their mother
    felt the same addiction and simply never spoke of
    it. The lesson they rejected: "You cannot save
    everyone, Ren. Some pain is meant to be felt."
  • Rival: The Hollow Council  They sent a tracker
    after Ren, not out of malice, but obligation.
    A mender who leaves is considered "unresolved
    pain" in the community. The tracker, Briar, was
    once Ren's closest friend.

 🎣 DM HOOKS
  • Secret: Ren has been deliberately seeking out
    wounded strangers not out of compassion, but
    because they need the fix. The party's injuries
    are a source of relief for Ren  and guilt.
  • Unresolved Thread: Lira has sent three letters
    that Ren has not opened. The last one was thicker
    than the others  it may contain more than words.
  • Evolution Arc: Ren must learn to feel without
    absorbing. True healing starts when they stop
    using others' pain as a substitute for their own
    emotions  and finally grieve what they lost.
  • Cultural Touchstone: In Wildborne circles, the
    Ash-Lamp vigil names the dead at moonrise  a rite
    Ren has avoided since leaving home.


```
