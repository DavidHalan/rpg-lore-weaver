# Sample Character: Tormenta 20

> Example output generated by `rpg-lore-weaver` for a Tormenta 20 campaign set in Arton, with a high-fantasy-with-drama tone.

```


════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  YNARA SOLQUEIMADA — The Brand She Didn't Choose
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

 📌 ELEVATOR PITCH
   A Lefou tracker who hunts Tormenta aberrations
   with terrifying efficiency  because the storm
   already lives inside her, and she refuses to
   let it have the last word.

 👤 APPEARANCE & VIBE
   Tall and lean, with sun-darkened skin marked by
   faint crimson veins that pulse under stress
   the Tormenta's mark, impossible to hide. Keeps
   her left arm wrapped in leather bindings at all
   times; the skin underneath is textured like
   chitin. Shaved head, practical  less for
   aesthetics, more because she got tired of the
   red streaks growing in her hair. Moves with
   the quiet precision of someone who has spent
   years tracking things that don't want to be
   found. Wears a necklace of small animal bones
    each one from a creature she killed that had
   been corrupted. Her voice is low, measured, and
   she pauses too long before answering questions,
   as if checking that the response is hers and
   not something else speaking through her.

 📖 BACKSTORY
   Ynara was born in a small village at the edge
   of the Tormenta's influence in northern Arton,
   where the red storms touched the horizon every
   night. Her parents were farmers  stubborn
   people who refused to leave because "the land
   was here before the storm, and it'll be here
   after." They were wrong.

   When Ynara was twelve, a Tormenta surge hit the
   village. Not a full invasion  worse. A slow
   corruption. Animals changed first: cattle grew
   extra limbs, dogs stopped blinking. Then the
   crops. Then the water. Her father was the first
   person to show the marks. Red veins on his arms.
   He hid them for weeks.

   By the time the Defensores de Arton arrived,
   half the village was gone  consumed, corrupted,
   or fled. Ynara's father attacked the soldiers.
   He didn't recognize his own daughter anymore.
   A Defendor named Commandante Braga made the
   decision. Ynara watched.

   Braga found Ynara sitting in the ashes three
   days later. She hadn't moved. She hadn't eaten.
   But she had mapped, in the dirt with a stick,
   every path the corruption had taken through
   the village  water sources, wind patterns,
   animal migration. She was twelve, and she had
   reverse-engineered a Tormenta surge.

   Braga took her in. Not out of pity  out of
   strategic interest. Ynara had the mark on her
   left arm, faint but undeniable. She was touched
   by the storm but not taken. Braga called her
   "the exception." The Defensores called her
   "the half-turned." Ynara called herself nothing
   for a long time.

   She trained as a tracker  someone who maps
   Tormenta activity, predicts surges, and hunts
   corrupted creatures before they reach populated
   areas. She is exceptional at it. The mark helps.
   She can feel the storm's patterns like weather
   in her bones. She hates that she can.

 🧠 PSYCHOLOGICAL PROFILE
  • Personality: Quiet, observant, and
    uncomfortably direct. Doesn't soften bad news.
    Doesn't small-talk. When she trusts someone,
    it shows not in words but in proximity  she
    sits closer, checks their equipment, saves
    them food. Permits herself exactly one luxury:
    she carves small wooden animals in the
    evenings, always of creatures that no longer
    exist in her homeland.
  • Ideals: The Tormenta is not a force of nature
     it is an invasion, and every corrupted
    creature was once something innocent. She
    fights not out of hatred for the storm, but
    out of grief for what it takes.
  • Bonds: Commandante Braga, who raised her and
    trained her. He's aging now, slowing down,
    and she pretends not to notice. The necklace
    of bones  each one is a prayer, though she
    won't say to which Divindade. Her village,
    which no longer exists on any map.
  • Flaws: Cannot trust anyone who hasn't seen the
    Tormenta firsthand. Dismisses "city people"
    who talk about the storm in abstract terms.
    Checks her own arm obsessively, measuring the
    spread of the marks. Has contingency plans for
    what to do "if it takes me"  plans she's
    shared with Braga and no one else.
  • Fear: That the mark is not a random corruption
    but a connection. That the storm chose her.
    That her ability to track it isn't skill  it's
    the Tormenta calling its own home.
  • Desire vs Need: WANTS to eradicate every trace
    of the Tormenta from Arton, burn it clean, and
    prove that the storm has no claim on her. NEEDS
    to accept that her mark is part of who she is
     not a disease to cure, but a wound that made
    her stronger.

  Divindade: Privately drawn to Allihanna (nature,
  animals) but refuses formal Devoção  she's seen
  too many devout people lose everything.

 🔗 CONNECTIONS
  • Ally: Commandante Braga  Her mentor, adoptive
    father figure, and the man who killed her
    biological father. She has never discussed this
    with him. He has never apologized. They both
    know. It sits between them like a third person
    at every meal.
  • Mentor: Scholar Deva of the Ordem da Luz  An
    elderly researcher who studies the Tormenta's
    patterns. She corresponds with Ynara by letter,
    exchanging data for field observations. Deva
    treats Ynara's mark as a research subject with
    clinical detachment, which Ynara simultaneously
    appreciates and resents.
  • Rival: Torren Valcrest  A paladin of Khalmyr
    who believes every Lefou is a threat, regardless
    of allegiance. He has argued publicly for Ynara
    to be "contained." He's not wrong about the
    danger  he's just wrong about her. They've
    worked the same missions twice. Both times, he
    watched her more than the enemy.

 🎣 DM HOOKS
  • Secret: Ynara has been tracking the spread of
    her own marks with the same precision she uses
    for Tormenta surges. She has a journal with
    monthly measurements. The rate of spread has
    increased since she started taking more
    dangerous missions. She suspects the correlation.
    She hasn't stopped.
  • Unresolved Thread: Three months ago, during a
    mission in a Tormenta-corrupted cave, Ynara
    heard a voice. Not words  the same shapeless
    communication the storm uses. But this one said
    something she understood: her mother's name.
    Her mother, who was never found after the surge.
    Not dead, not corrupted  just gone.
  • Evolution Arc: Ynara must choose between
    destroying the part of herself connected to the
    storm (losing her tracking abilities and her
    greatest weapon) or embracing it fully (gaining
    power but risking exactly what she fears most).
    The middle path  using the connection without
    being consumed  requires something she's
    never attempted: trusting the mark instead of
    fighting it.
  • Cultural Touchstone: In Tollon, hunters still tell
    the folktale of the Lantern of Aharadak, making
    Ynara's scar a public myth as much as a private fear.
```
