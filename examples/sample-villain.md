# Sample Villain: D&D 5e

> Example output generated by `rpg-lore-weaver` using `references/villain-mode.md` for a D&D 5e campaign.

```


🗡️ VILLAIN PROFILE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  VERATH SUNHOLLOW — The Gardener of Flesh

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

 📌 ELEVATOR PITCH
   A wood elf druid who watched her sacred grove
   die from a magical plague and concluded that
   nature's greatest flaw is its fragility — so
   she's replacing every living thing in the
   Greenvale with hardier, aberrant versions that
   can never wilt, never rot, and never die, whether
   they want to or not.

 🪞 THE MIRROR (Motivation)
  • The Wound: The Greenvale Blight — a necrotic
    plague engineered by a lich (now destroyed)
    killed her grove, her animal companions, and
    the dryad she considered her sister. Verath
    survived because she was away, trading seeds
    at a market. She returned to a forest of ash.
  • The Logic: "Nature is beautiful but weak. One
    plague, one fire, one drought — and centuries
    of growth vanish overnight. If I make things
    that CANNOT die, the cycle of loss ends forever.
    Pain ends. Grief ends." She's not wrong about
    the diagnosis. She's catastrophically wrong
    about the cure.
  • The Line: Her first "improvement" was a deer.
    She fused it with fungal tissue to make it
    unkillable. It screamed for three days before
    the pain receptors finally died. She told
    herself it was "adaptation discomfort." She
    hasn't stopped telling herself that.
  • Push vs Pull: PULLED toward a vision where
    nothing can be lost. The grief is behind her;
    the utopia is ahead. She doesn't look back.

 📋 THE PLAN
  • Goal: Transform the entire Greenvale ecosystem
    into self-sustaining aberrant life — plants,
    animals, and eventually people — immune to
    disease, aging, and death.
  • Method: A network of "seed altars" — corrupted
    druidic circles that release spores converting
    organic matter within a 1-mile radius. She has
    planted six. She needs twelve for the Greenvale.
    After that: the continent.
  • Timeline: 3 altars active now (Phase 1: local
    wildlife converted). By spring: 6 active (Phase
    2: forest fully converted). By year's end: 12
    (Phase 3: spores reach farmland and villages).
    Unchecked: within 5 years, irreversible.
  • Resources: A circle of 4 disillusioned druids
    who share her grief. A myconid colony she didn't
    corrupt — they joined willingly, seeing her
    vision as evolution. Three shambling mounds she
    grew from the converted forest, serving as both
    guardians and seed-carriers.

 💔 THE CRACKS
  • Blind Spot: She cannot see that her "gifts"
    cause suffering. The screaming deer was an
    anomaly, she insists. The converted animals'
    hollow eyes are "serenity, not emptiness."
  • The One Thing She Protects: A single cutting
    from the original Life-Tree of her grove — the
    ONLY piece of the old Greenvale she kept natural.
    It sits in a glass terrarium in her sanctum.
    She talks to it. If destroyed, her conviction
    cracks.
  • The Tell: She hums a lullaby when she's about
    to corrupt something — the same lullaby her
    dryad sister used to sing. She doesn't realize
    she does this.
  • Redemption: 3/5 — Verath is not malicious. She
    is a grieving healer who decided that preventing
    ALL death is worth any cost. If confronted with
    the suffering her creations endure (not told —
    SHOWN), she might break.

 🔗 WEB (Relationship to PCs)
  • Connection: One of the PCs' home villages is
    at the edge of the Greenvale. The first "improved"
    animals have been spotted in the fields.
  • Temptation: She can cure any disease, heal any
    wound, reverse any death — if the recipient
    accepts the conversion. "You'll never feel pain
    again. Isn't that what you want?"
  • Attitude: Genuine compassion. She doesn't hate
    the PCs. She pities them. "You still grieve.
    I can fix that."

 🎣 DM HOOKS
  • Secret: The lich who created the Blight didn't
    target the Greenvale randomly. Verath's grove
    was built over a sealed portal to the Far Realm.
    The lich wanted it open. Verath's aberrant
    experiments are accidentally finishing the lich's
    work — the converted ecosystem is thinning
    the barrier.
  • Escalation Beat: One of Verath's druids defects
    and contacts the PCs, bringing a converted
    songbird in a cage. The bird is alive, healthy,
    and utterly silent. It hasn't blinked in four
    days. The druid whispers: "She says they're
    at peace. Look at it. Does that look like peace?"
  • Moral Dilemma: A child in the PC's village is
    dying of the same plague that killed Verath's
    grove (dormant spores she accidentally spread).
    Verath offers the cure — conversion. The parents
    are willing. The child is afraid. What do the
    PCs do?


```
