# D&D 5e / 5.5e Rules Summary (Backstory Focus)

## Key Concepts

### Race / Lineage

- **Implication**: Biology and culture combined. Explore how the character feels about their heritage.
- **Common Options**: Human, Elf, Dwarf, Halfling, Tiefling, Dragonborn, Gnome, Half-Orc, Half-Elf, Aasimar, Genasi, Goliath.

### Class

- **Implication**: The "job" or "calling" — deeply tied to identity.
- **Martial** (Fighter, Barbarian, Rogue, Monk): Training, survival, discipline, or rebellion?
- **Magic** (Wizard, Sorcerer, Warlock, Artificer): Study, bloodline, pact, or invention?
- **Divine** (Cleric, Paladin, Druid, Ranger): Faith, oath, nature, or duty?

### Background

- **Mechanical**: Gives skill proficiencies and a feature.
- **Narrative**: The life they lived _before_ adventuring. This is the seed of Phase 1.
- **Common Backgrounds**: Acolyte, Criminal, Folk Hero, Noble, Outlander, Sage, Soldier, Urchin, Hermit, Charlatan, Entertainer, Guild Artisan.

### Alignment

- **Structure**: Lawful/Neutral/Chaotic vs Good/Neutral/Evil.
- **Usage**: Treat as a _moral tendency_, not a straitjacket. Best used as internal tension.

## Backstory by Class Category

### Martial Classes

**Fighter / Barbarian / Rogue / Monk**

- Who trained you to fight, and what did they demand in return?
- What battle or moment convinced you that violence was necessary?
- What code or principle do you follow, even when it costs you?
- What weapon or fighting style carries emotional significance?

### Arcane Classes

**Wizard / Sorcerer / Warlock / Artificer**

- How did you discover your magical ability? Was it chosen or forced?
- What was the first spell you cast, and what were the consequences?
- Who fears your power? Who covets it?
- (Warlock) What did your pact cost you that you haven't paid yet?

### Divine Classes

**Cleric / Paladin / Druid / Ranger**

- What moment crystallized your faith, oath, or bond with nature?
- Have you ever doubted your purpose? What happened?
- Who do you serve, and what happens if you fail them?
- What sacred place or ritual grounds you when the world shakes?

### Skill Classes

**Bard / Ranger / Rogue**

- Where did you learn your trade, and who was your first audience/mark/prey?
- What story, performance, or heist defined your reputation?
- What secret do you carry that would ruin your reputation if revealed?

## Connections Prompts

Use these to build bonds between PCs and NPCs:

- Who saved your life, and what do you owe them?
- Who did you wrong, and do they know?
- Who do you write letters to that never respond?
- What mentor's voice do you hear in moments of doubt?
- What rival pushes you to be better (or worse)?

## Mechanical Suggestions (Phase 6)

After the narrative is complete, suggest these D&D-specific mechanics:

### Ability Scores

Recommend which scores should be highest based on backstory:

- Strength: Physical labor, combat training, survival
- Dexterity: Stealth, acrobatics, fine work
- Constitution: Hardship, endurance, resilience
- Intelligence: Study, observation, strategy
- Wisdom: Faith, intuition, empathy
- Charisma: Leadership, deception, performance

### Ideals, Bonds, and Flaws (5e)

Derive directly from backstory — never use generic PHB text:

- **Ideal**: The core belief that drives action → from Phase 2 (Engine)
- **Bond**: The person/place/thing that anchors them → from Phase 4 (Web)
- **Flaw**: The weakness that creates drama → from Phase 3 (Cracks)

### Proficiencies & Skills

Suggest skills based on life experience described in backstory. Example: a character raised in a forest should have Nature/Survival, not Arcana.

### Background Feature

Choose the background whose feature best matches the narrative, regardless of thematic label. A "Noble" background works for a fallen aristocrat or a con artist who stole a title.

### Inspiration Triggers (5.5e)

Suggest specific moments that should earn Inspiration: "When Kael defends a non-human against prejudice" > "When acting heroic."

## World Assumptions (Generic)

- High magic is common; gods are real and active
- Adventurers are exceptional individuals
- The Forgotten Realms is the default setting, but always confirm with the player
- D&D 5.5e (2024) updates lineage rules — ask which version the player uses
