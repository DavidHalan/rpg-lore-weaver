# Call of Cthulhu 7th Edition Rules Summary (Backstory Focus)

## Key Concepts

### Investigators (not Heroes)

- **Implication**: Characters are ordinary people encountering the extraordinary. They are fragile, mortal, and expendable.
- **Tone**: Investigative horror. Knowledge is dangerous. Curiosity kills.

### Occupations

The character's profession defines their skills and social standing:

- **Academic**: Professor, Librarian, Scientist, Doctor, Archaeologist
- **Law & Order**: Police Detective, Private Investigator, Lawyer, Federal Agent
- **Creative**: Author, Journalist, Artist, Photographer, Filmmaker
- **Labor**: Mechanic, Sailor, Farmer, Soldier, Nurse
- **Fringe**: Antiquarian, Occultist, Drifter, Criminal, Clergy

> Each occupation provides a skill list. The occupation chosen should emerge from the backstory, not the other way around.

### Sanity

- **Starting Sanity = POW × 5**. This is the character's grip on reality.
- **Narrative**: Every encounter with the Mythos chips away at this. The backstory should establish what "normal" means to the character — so the horror of losing it hits harder.

## Backstory Entries (Official — Create All 10)

The CoC 7e rules require 10 backstory elements. Use the character's narrative to fill each:

1. **Personal Description**: Physical appearance, mannerisms, how others perceive them
   - _Examples_: Rugged, Alluring, Bookish, Dashing, Scruffy, Serene, Wolfish, Nimble, Slovenly, Vivacious
2. **Ideology/Beliefs**: Religious faith, political stance, philosophical view, or superstition
   - _Examples_: "Everyone let me down, so I take care of myself." / "There is no higher power than truth." / "It's all about community, working together." / "My destiny is already decided."
3. **Significant People**: Who matters most — pick WHO and WHY:
   - _Who_: Parent, Grandparent, Sibling, Child, Partner, Mentor, Friend, Famous Person, Fellow Investigator, Enemy
   - _Why_: Indebted, Taught something, Gives life meaning, You wronged them, Shared experience, Seek to prove yourself, Idolize, Regret, Rivalry, Revenge
4. **Meaningful Locations**: A place that feels like home, or a place that haunts them
5. **Treasured Possessions**: An object they'd risk their life for
   - _Examples_: Item connected to highest skill, Occupation essential, Childhood memento, Departed person's memento, Gift from significant person, Your collection, Mysterious found object, Sporting item, Weapon, Pet
6. **Traits**: Personality trait that defines first impressions
   - _Examples_: Generous, Dreamer, Loyal, Fearless, Impulsive, Trusting, Greedy, Cautious, Skeptical, Honest, Cowardly, Risk-taker, Ambitious
7. **Injuries & Scars**: Physical or psychological marks from the past
8. **Phobias & Manias**: Pre-existing fears or obsessions (before the Mythos gets involved)
9. **Arcane Tomes, Spells & Artifacts**: Any prior exposure to the occult (usually none at start)
10. **Encounters with Strange Entities**: Prior brushes with the unexplained (optional, risky)

> **Key Backstory Connection**: Choose ONE of the above (usually #3 or #4) as the character's anchor — the thing that keeps them sane when reality breaks.

## Connections Prompts

- Who would you call at 3 AM if something impossible happened?
- Who do you owe money to, and what happens if you don't pay?
- Who from your past would you rather forget, and why can't you?
- What professional colleague do you respect but disagree with fundamentally?
- Who depends on you being "normal"?

## Mechanical Suggestions (Phase 6)

After the narrative is complete, suggest these CoC-specific mechanics:

### Characteristics (Attributes)

Derive from backstory — CoC uses: STR, CON, SIZ, DEX, APP, INT, POW, EDU.

- **POW (Power/Willpower)**: Derive from the character's inner resolve and beliefs
- **EDU (Education)**: Derive from their academic/life experience background
- **APP (Appearance)**: Derive from physical description and social presence

### Occupation & Credit Rating

Choose the occupation that best matches the character's described profession. Credit Rating reflects social status and financial stability.

### Key Skills

Suggest 8-10 skills that match the character's occupation AND backstory:

- A professor should have Library Use, History, Persuade
- A war veteran should have Firearms, First Aid, Dodge
- A journalist should have Fast Talk, Photography, Spot Hidden

### Luck

In CoC 7e, Luck is rolled but narratively crucial. Low Luck characters create more dramatic stories.

### Backstory Anchor

Identify which of the 10 backstory entries serves as the **Key Backstory Connection** — the emotional anchor that the Keeper can use to drive horror.

## World Assumptions

- Set in the 1920s (Classic) by default — ask the player for era preference
- The Cthulhu Mythos is real and incomprehensible
- Magic exists but always comes at a cost (Sanity)
- Characters are mortal; death and madness are expected outcomes
- Investigation drives the plot, not combat
