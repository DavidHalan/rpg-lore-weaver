# Pathfinder 2e Rules Summary (Backstory Focus)

## Key Concepts

### Ancestry

- **Implication**: More than biology — includes heritage and culture. Ancestry feats represent cultural skills passed down.
- **Common Ancestries**: Human, Elf, Dwarf, Gnome, Halfling, Goblin, Leshy, Orc, Kobold, Catfolk, Tengu, Fetchling, Kitsune.
- **Heritage**: A sub-selection within ancestry (e.g., Arctic Elf, Gutsy Halfling) that adds a cultural or biological layer.
- **Versatile Heritages**: Half-Elf, Half-Orc, Aasimar, Tiefling, Dhampir, Changeling — for characters caught between two worlds.

### Background

- **Mechanical**: Provides 2 attribute boosts, skill training, and a lore skill.
- **Narrative**: Represents what the character did BEFORE adventuring. This is the seed of Phase 1.
- **Examples**: Barkeep, Bounty Hunter, Criminal, Emissary, Farmhand, Gladiator, Hermit, Martial Disciple, Merchant, Nomad, Scholar, Scout, Street Urchin, Warrior.

### Class

Pathfinder 2e classes are highly customizable through **class feats** that shape playstyle:

- **Martial**: Fighter, Barbarian, Ranger, Rogue, Monk, Swashbuckler, Gunslinger, Inventor
- **Spellcasting**: Wizard, Sorcerer, Witch, Bard, Cleric, Druid, Oracle, Psychic, Summoner, Kineticist
- **Hybrid**: Champion, Magus, Investigator, Thaumaturge

### Edicts & Anathema

- **Edicts**: Things the character is compelled to do by code, faith, or personal vow.
- **Anathema**: Things the character MUST NOT do — violating these has mechanical and narrative consequences.
- **Usage**: Mandatory for divine classes (Champion, Cleric), but excellent for ALL characters as narrative guardrails.
- **Key Question**: "What will your character always do? What will they never do, even at great cost?"

## Backstory Prompts

### By Ancestry

- How does your heritage shape how others treat you? Do you resent or embrace it?
- What tradition from your ancestry do you still practice? Which have you abandoned?
- What is the one thing your people are unfairly judged for?

### By Background

- What event in your previous career pushed you toward adventuring?
- What skill from your old life saves you more often than your combat abilities?
- Who from your previous profession do you still owe a debt to?

### By Class

**Martial:**

- Who trained you, and what philosophy of combat did they instill?
- What fight changed your understanding of violence?
- What weapon or technique carries personal meaning beyond utility?

**Spellcasting:**

- How did magic first manifest in your life? Was it welcome?
- What spell have you sworn never to cast again, and why?
- Who is afraid of your power? Are they wrong to be?

**Hybrid:**

- Why does a single discipline feel insufficient for your purpose?
- What internal conflict drives you to blend two approaches?
- What oath or bond defines the boundaries of your power?

### Edicts & Anathema Prompts

- Finish: "I will always **\_** because **\_**."
- Finish: "I will never **\_** because **\_**."
- What moral compromise keeps you awake at night?
- What would your mentor say is your greatest failure of principle?

## Connections Prompts

- Who gave you your first piece of adventuring equipment, and what does it mean?
- What ally do you compete with for the same goal?
- Who in Golarion would shelter you no questions asked? Why?
- What rival shares your background but took a different path?
- Who do you write to when you need to remember who you were before?

## Mechanical Suggestions (Phase 6)

### Attribute Boosts

PF2e distributes boosts from Ancestry, Background, Class, and 4 free. Suggest:

- Which boosts come from story (e.g., a farmhand → STR/CON from labor)
- Which boosts reflect class needs vs narrative truth
- Flaws based on backstory weaknesses (a scholar → low STR makes sense)

### Ancestry Feats

Choose feats that reflect the character's cultural upbringing, not just optimization:

- An elf raised among humans might skip elven weapon familiarity
- A goblin who overcame prejudice might take "Burn It!" ironically

### Background Skill & Lore

Choose the Lore skill that matches the specific backstory: Warfare Lore, Underworld Lore, Herbalism Lore, Accounting Lore — specificity is better.

### Edicts & Anathema

Derive directly from backstory:

- Edicts from Phase 2 (Engine) ideals + Phase 4 (Web) loyalty
- Anathema from Phase 3 (Cracks) trauma + personal red lines

### Class Feats

Suggest 1st-level class feats that reinforce the narrative identity. Example: A rogue who grew up as a street urchin might take "Nimble Dodge" (survival instinct) over "Twin Feint" (trained technique).

## World Assumptions (Golarion)

- High fantasy with deep lore and diverse cultures
- Gods are real, active, and organized (pantheon structure with edicts/anathema)
- Ancestries have rich cultural histories that shape identity
- The world is dangerous — characters earn their power through experience
- Pathfinder emphasizes player agency through extensive customization
