## D&D 5e / 5.5e

**Key terms**: Background, Alignment, Bond, Flaw, Ideal, Trait

**Discovery extras** Ask after standard questions:

- What **Background** did you choose? (Acolyte, Criminal, Noble, etc.)
- Does the character follow an **Alignment**, or do you prefer to let it emerge?
- Are you playing in an **established setting** (Forgotten Realms, Eberron, Ravenloft) or homebrew?

**Phase 1 extras**:

- D&D Backgrounds come with a suggested origin. Use it as a **starting seed**, not the whole story.
- Ask: "The Background says you're a [Sage]. But _why_? Was it by choice, necessity, or accident?"
- Ask: "Your race gives you certain traits. How does your character _feel_ about those traits?"

**Phase 2 extras**:

- D&D has Bonds, Flaws, and Ideals baked in. Use them as **prompts, not answers**.
- Ask: "The PHB suggests [ideal]. Does your character actually believe this, or is it what they _want_ to believe?"
- Ask: "What would make your character break their alignment?"

**Phase 3 extras**:

- Connect low ability scores to narrative. A Barbarian with INT 8 isn't "dumb" they might be _wise in ways books can't teach_.
- Ask: "What happened to give you that 8 in [stat]?"

**Phase 4 extras**:

- Ask: "Does your character know anyone in the setting? A mentor from a guild? A rival from training?"
- D&D is rich with factions (Harpers, Zhentarim, Lords' Alliance). Ask: "Does any faction interest your character?"

**Output adaptation**:

- Include **Alignment** in the Psychological Profile (but frame it as tendency, not prison)
- Include **Background feature** and how the character uses it narratively

## Pathfinder 2e

**Key terms**: Ancestry, Heritage, Background, Class, Key Ability, Deity

**Discovery extras**:

- What **Ancestry** and **Heritage** did you choose? (e.g., Half-Elf with Cavern Elf heritage)
- Do you follow a **deity**? Which one and why?
- Pathfinder has _Edicts_ and _Anathema_ for some classes. Do these apply?

**Phase 1 extras**:

- Pathfinder Ancestries have rich lore. Ask: "How does your character relate to their Ancestry's culture?"
- Ask: "Golarion is a big world. Which region shaped your childhood? Absalom's cosmopolitan bustle? The Mwangi Expanse's wild beauty?"
- Heritage adds nuance: "Your Half-Elf heritage do you feel more Elf or more Human? Or neither?"

**Phase 2 extras**:

- Pathfinder's **Edicts and Anathema** are great motivation tools
- Ask: "What Edict does your character follow willingly? Which one chafes?"
- Ask: "If your deity asked you to do something against your morals, what would you do?"

**Phase 3 extras**:

- Pathfinder's ancestry feats can inform narrative. A Dwarf with _Forge-Day's Rest_ has a story about why rest matters.
- Ask: "What is the hardest lesson your training taught you?"

**Phase 4 extras**:

- Pathfinder has rich NPC organizations. Ask about Pathfinder Society, Knights of Lastwall, Firebrands, etc.
- Ask: "Who trained you in your class? Were they kind, harsh, or indifferent?"

**Output adaptation**:

- Include **Deity** and relationship with them
- Include **Edicts/Anathema** in Ideals section
- Use Pathfinder-specific terminology (Ancestry instead of Race)

## Daggerheart

**Key terms**: Community, Ancestry, Class, Subclass, Domain, Evasion/Armor

**Discovery extras**:

- What **Community** did you grow up in?
- Daggerheart is narrative-first. How comfortable is the player with _improvising in character_?
- Does the player want a character that _embraces_ Daggerheart's Hope/Fear mechanic narratively?

**Phase 1 extras**:

- Daggerheart Communities are looser than D&D Backgrounds. Use this freedom.
- Ask: "Describe your Community in three sensory details a sound, a smell, a sight."
- Ask: "What was the one rule in your Community that everyone followed? Did your character agree with it?"

**Phase 2 extras**:

- Daggerheart's Domains shape abilities AND worldview. Ask: "Your Domain is [Arcana/Blade/etc.]. How did you discover this affinity?"
- The Hope/Fear mechanic is narratively rich. Ask: "When Fear takes over, what does your character do? Freeze? Lash out? Retreat?"

**Phase 3 extras**:

- Daggerheart characters are meant to be _vulnerable_. Lean into this.
- Ask: "What is one thing your character is terrible at and refuses to admit?"
- Ask: "Daggerheart is about hope in dark times. What keeps your character hoping?"

**Phase 4 extras**:

- Daggerheart's party dynamics are central. Ask: "How does your character feel about relying on others?"
- Ask: "Who in your Community do you think about when you're scared?"

**Output adaptation**:

- Include **Community** description
- Frame Desire vs Need through Hope/Fear lens
- Keep language more narrative, less mechanical

## Call of Cthulhu

**Key terms**: Occupation, Backstory, Sanity, Connections, Key Contacts, Phobias, Beliefs

**Discovery extras**:

- What **era** are you playing? (1890s, 1920s, Modern)
- What is your character's **Occupation**?
- How much does your character know about the _supernatural_ at the start?

**Phase 1 extras**:

- CoC characters are _ordinary people_. Lean into normalcy before horror.
- Ask: "Describe a perfectly normal Tuesday for your character before everything went wrong."
- Ask: "What is the most mundane thing your character loves? Fresh-baked bread? A specific radio show? Their garden?"

**Phase 2 extras**:

- CoC motivation is often _curiosity that shouldn't be indulged_.
- Ask: "Why can't your character just walk away when things get strange?"
- Ask: "What do they believe in? God? Science? Human decency? How brittle is that belief?"

**Phase 3 extras**:

- Sanity is the core mechanic. Ask: "What would break your character's mind? What coping mechanism do they rely on?"
- Ask: "What phobia or superstition did they have _before_ encountering the Mythos?"
- Ask: "What is the one thing they cannot lose without falling apart?"

**Phase 4 extras**:

- CoC characters need **anchors** people who keep them tethered to reality.
- Ask: "Who is the person your character calls when they can't sleep at 3 AM?"
- Ask: "Who depends on your character? That's the reason they can't just run away."

**Output adaptation**:

- Replace "DM Hooks" with **"Keeper Hooks"**
- Add **Sanity Anchors** section (people/things that restore SAN)
- Include **Pre-Mythos beliefs** and how they'll be tested
- Tone should be understated, literary, creeping dread

## Tormenta 20

**Key terms**: Raça, Classe, Divindade, Devoção, Poder Concedido, Tormenta (the cosmic storm)

**Discovery extras**:

- What **Raça** (race) did you choose? Tormenta has unique races: Lefou, Qareen, Minotauro, Golem, Hynne, Sílfide, Aggelus, Sulfure
- Do you follow a **Divindade** from the Panteão? (Khalmyr, Thyatis, Nimb, Tenebra, Allihanna, Valkaria, etc.)
- How does the character feel about the **Tormenta**? Have they seen its effects?

**Phase 1 extras**:

- Arton is a world with rich history. Ask: "Which kingdom or region are you from? The technological Vectora? The deserts of Galrasia? The chaotic Cidade de Nimb?"
- Ask: "Has the Tormenta affected your homeland? How did your family deal with it?"
- For Lefou: "You carry the mark of the Tormenta. How do people react? How do YOU react?"
- For Qareen: "Your inner desire what is it? How does it guide your decisions?"

**Phase 2 extras**:

- Devoção (devotion) is central in Tormenta. Ask: "Is your relationship with your Divindade genuine faith, a utilitarian exchange, or fear?"
- Ask: "Nimb is the god of chaos and luck. Has your character ever felt 'blessed' by chance? Or cursed?"
- Ask: "Valkaria ascended from mortal to goddess. Does this inspire or frighten your character?"

**Phase 3 extras**:

- The Tormenta physically corrupts. Ask: "Has your character ever been tempted by the Tormenta? Have they seen someone give in?"
- Ask: "What was the greatest loss the Tormenta caused in your life?"
- Ask: "Is there a decision your character made out of desperation rather than courage?"

**Phase 4 extras**:

- Ask: "Does your character belong to any organization? The Ordem da Luz? The Defensores de Arton? The Protetorado do Reich?"
- Ask: "Who was your master or mentor? Are they still alive?"
- Ask: "Do you have any rivalry with followers of an opposing Divindade?"

**Output adaptation**:

- Include **Divindade** and type of Devoção (fervent, questioning, reluctant)
- Include **Relationship with the Tormenta** fear, hatred, fascination, indifference
- Use setting terminology: "reinos" instead of "nations", "Poderes Concedidos" instead of "blessings"
- Tone adapts: Tormenta blends high fantasy with humor and drama maintain that balance

## Ordem Paranormal

**Key terms**: Agente, Ordo Realitas, NEX (Paranormal Exposure), Sanidade (Sanity), Ritual, O Outro Lado (The Other Side), Elemento (Sangue, Morte, Energia, Conhecimento, Medo)

**Discovery extras**:

- What is the agent's **classe**? (Combatente, Especialista, Ocultista)
- What is their starting **NEX**? How much contact have they had with O Outro Lado?
- Did the character have any notion of the paranormal before being recruited, or was the Ordo a total shock?

**Phase 1 extras**:

- Agents of the Ordem are ordinary people who were recruited. Ask: "What was your life BEFORE the Ordo Realitas?"
- Ask: "How were you recruited? Did you witness something? Did someone refer you? Did you survive an encounter?"
- Ask: "Where in Brazil did you grow up? How does that affect how you deal with the supernatural?"
- Ask: "You had a normal life job, college, family. What did you leave behind when you joined the Ordem?"

**Phase 2 extras**:

- The Ordo demands absolute secrecy. Ask: "How do you cope with not being able to tell the truth to the people you love?"
- Ask: "What motivates you to keep going? Justice? Curiosity? Fear that if you stop, no one else will do it?"
- Ask: "Do you use Rituais? How do you feel about using something that shouldn't exist?"
- Ask: "Paranormal Elementos come at a cost. What price do you fear paying most?"

**Phase 3 extras**:

- Rising NEX corrupts. Ask: "As your NEX increases, what do you notice changing about yourself?"
- Ask: "Does O Outro Lado call to you? In dreams? In reflections? What do you see?"
- Ask: "Which mission affected you the most? The one that made you question whether it's worth it?"
- Ask: "Have you ever lost Sanidade in the field? What happened? How did you recover (or didn't)?"

**Phase 4 extras**:

- Ask: "Who in your group would you protect with your own life? Who don't you fully trust?"
- Ask: "Do you have a contact outside the Ordo who knows everything? Or are you completely isolated?"
- Ask: "Who was your mentor in the Ordem? What happened to their previous team?"
- Ask: "Is there someone from your past who could be used against you by an entity?"

**Output adaptation**:

- Replace fantasy terminology: "DM" **"Mestre"**, "campaign" **"missão/campanha"**
- Include **NEX and Sanidade** as part of the Psychological Profile
- Include **Relationship with O Outro Lado** fear, fascination, denial, acceptance
- Include **Life before the Ordo** as a fundamental part of the Backstory
- DM Hooks **"Ganchos pro Mestre"**: secrets, interested entities, loose threads
- Tone: **Brazilian investigative horror** urban, tense, with moments of humanity amid chaos

## System-Agnostic

When the user chooses no system or an unlisted one:

- Skip all mechanical questions (stats, classes, backgrounds)
- Focus entirely on narrative pillars
- Use generic terms: "abilities" instead of "stats", "role" instead of "class"
- Ask: "What genre is your story? Fantasy? Sci-fi? Horror? Modern?"
- Ask: "Is there magic/technology in your world? How common is it?"
- Ask: "What's the power structure? Monarchy? Corporations? Tribes? Anarchy?"

## Tone Vocabulary Guide

Adapt word choice based on campaign tone:

| Tone                     | Language Style                   | Example Description                                                    |
| ------------------------ | -------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **High Fantasy**         | Grand, poetic, hopeful           | "A blade forged in starlight, carried by one who still dares to dream" |
| **Grimdark**             | Blunt, visceral, pessimistic     | "A notched sword and a gut full of cheap ale that's all that's left"   |
| **Comedic**              | Playful, exaggerated, self-aware | "A wizard who keeps accidentally setting their eyebrows on fire"       |
| **Intrigue**             | Precise, layered, ambiguous      | "A diplomat whose smile never quite reaches their eyes"                |
| **Horror**               | Restrained, sensory, unsettling  | "Something shifted in the cellar. Just once. Just enough."             |
| **Investigative Horror** | Urban, tense, grounded           | "The reflection in the mirror blinked. You didn't."                    |
