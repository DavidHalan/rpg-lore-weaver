# System Conversion Guide

How to convert a character concept between different RPG systems while preserving narrative identity. The mechanics change  the soul stays the same.

## Core Principle

> **The narrative pillars are system-agnostic. Only the mechanical expression changes.**

A character's Origin, Motivations, Flaws, and Connections work in ANY system. What changes is:

- How abilities are represented mechanically
- What vocabulary is used (class vs archetype vs occupation)
- What systemic pressures exist (alignment, sanity, corruption, etc.)
- What the world expects of them

## The Conversion Process

```
  Step 1: Extract the Narrative Core
          ↓
  Step 2: Map Mechanics to Target System
          ↓
  Step 3: Adapt Vocabulary and Tone
          ↓
  Step 4: Add System-Specific Elements
          ↓
  Step 5: Validate Consistency
```

### Step 1: Extract the Narrative Core

From the original character document, isolate:

- Elevator Pitch (system-neutral version)
- Core motivation (WANT vs NEED)
- Primary flaw
- Key relationships (ally, mentor, rival)
- The one-sentence theme of their arc

Everything else is mechanical and can be re-mapped.

### Step 2: Map Mechanics to Target System

Use the Concept Translation Tables below to find equivalents.

### Step 3: Adapt Vocabulary and Tone

Each system has its own language. A "Barbarian" in D&D might be a "Guerreiro" in Tormenta 20, a "Combatente" in Ordem Paranormal, or simply "a person who fights" in a narrative system like Daggerheart.

### Step 4: Add System-Specific Elements

Some systems have unique mechanics that don't exist in others:

- D&D  Alignment, Background feature
- Pathfinder  Edicts/Anathema, Ancestry feats
- Tormenta 20  Divindade, Devoção, Tormenta relationship
- Ordem Paranormal  NEX, Sanidade, O Outro Lado relationship
- Call of Cthulhu  Sanity anchors, pre-Mythos beliefs
- Daggerheart  Hope/Fear, Community

These MUST be created new  they can't be ported from another system.

### Step 5: Validate Consistency

Read the converted character document and ask:

- Does the Elevator Pitch still work?
- Are the motivations internally consistent?
- Do the connections still make sense in the new setting?
- Does the character FEEL the same, even if the details differ?

## Tone Adaptation

The same character feels different in different tonal contexts:

| Character Core        | High Fantasy (D&D/Tormenta)                  | Horror (CoC/Ordem Paranormal)                  | Narrative (Daggerheart)                             |
| --------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| "Protects the weak"   | Knights boldly, inspires others              | Protects desperately, can't save everyone      | Carries the weight of care, asks what it costs      |
| "Seeks knowledge"     | Explores ancient libraries, discovers wonder | Reads forbidden texts, pays in sanity          | Asks questions the world doesn't want answered      |
| "Haunted by the past" | Dramatic flashbacks, grand redemption        | Creeping dread, quiet spiral, mundane triggers | Tender memories that ache, small acts of healing    |
| "Doesn't belong"      | Proves worth through heroic deeds            | Isolation amplifies paranoia and vulnerability | Finds belonging in unexpected moments of connection |

## What to Keep vs What to Change

### Always Keep (Narrative Core)

-  Elevator Pitch (adapt wording, not meaning)
-  WANT vs NEED (universal)
-  Core flaw (the human weakness doesn't change)
-  Key relationships (the dynamics transfer)
-  The theme of their arc

### Always Change (System-Specific)

-  Class/role terminology
-  Mechanical abilities and stats
-  System-unique elements (alignment, sanity, NEX, etc.)
-  Setting-specific details (gods, organizations, locations)
-  Tone and vocabulary (match target system's feel)

### Case-by-Case

-  Backstory events (may need new setting equivalents)
-  NPC details (names and roles may change, dynamics shouldn't)
-  DM Hooks (adapt to system's adventure style)
