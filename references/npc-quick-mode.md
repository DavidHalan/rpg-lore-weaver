# NPC Quick Mode

A streamlined 3-pillar process for creating memorable NPCs in minutes, without the full 6-phase deep dive.

## When to Use

- DM needs an NPC **right now** during a session
- Creating a **cast of supporting characters** for a campaign
- Player wants to flesh out a **contact or family member** mentioned in backstory
- Quick prototype before deciding if the NPC deserves the full 10-pillar treatment

**Rule of thumb**: If the NPC will appear in more than 3 sessions, consider using the full 6-phase process instead.

---

## The 3 Pillars

The full skill uses 10 pillars. NPC Quick Mode distills them into **3 essential pillars**:

| Quick Pillar     | Covers                    | Full Pillars Condensed              |
| ---------------- | ------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| **1. The Hook**  | Who are they at a glance? | Origin + Personality + Appearance   |
| **2. The Want**  | What drives them?         | Motivations + Ideals + Key Decision |
| **3. The Crack** | What makes them human?    | Weakness + one key Connection       |

**Why 3?** Because players remember:

- _What someone looks like_ (Hook)
- _What they want_ (Want)
- _What makes them relatable_ (Crack)

Everything else is gravy.

## Quick Mode Workflow

### Step 1: Context (30 seconds)

Ask only:

1. **Who is this NPC to the party?** (shopkeeper, quest giver, villain, random encounter)
2. **One adjective** that describes them (nervous, jovial, menacing, motherly)
3. **System** (if not already established)

### Step 2: Generate the 3 Pillars (2-3 minutes)

For each pillar, ask ONE question and fill in the rest:

**Pillar 1 The Hook:**

- Ask: "What's the first thing a player notices about them?"
- Fill in: Name, appearance detail, voice/mannerism, vibe

**Pillar 2 The Want:**

- Ask: "What do they want from the party (or from life)?"
- Fill in: Surface motive, deeper motive (may differ), one thing they'd never do

**Pillar 3 The Crack:**

- Ask: "What's their one vulnerability?"
- Fill in: The flaw, how it shows, one person who matters to them

### Step 3: Output (30 seconds)

Generate the compact NPC card (see Output Format below).

**Total time: ~3-5 minutes**

## NPC Tiers

Not all NPCs need the same depth. Use these tiers to calibrate:

### Tier 1: Background (10 seconds)

NPCs that exist for atmosphere. No card needed.

- "The bartender wipes a glass and nods."
- "A child runs past carrying stolen bread."
- **Method**: One sentence. Name optional.

### Tier 2: Functional (1-2 minutes)

NPCs the party interacts with briefly.

- Shopkeepers, guards, innkeepers, messengers
- **Method**: Name + Hook + Want. Skip the Crack.

### Tier 3: Recurring (3-5 minutes) **Quick Mode default**

NPCs that will appear more than once.

- Quest givers, contacts, faction representatives, rivals
- **Method**: Full 3-pillar Quick Mode.

### Tier 4: Central (full skill)

NPCs that are critical to the campaign.

- Big bads, love interests, betrayers, mentors
- **Method**: Use the full 6-phase rpg-lore-weaver process.

## Rapid-Fire Generator

When the DM needs an NPC **immediately** (mid-session, no prep time), use this:

**Ask for 3 words**: Role, Adjective, Secret.

Example inputs:

- "Blacksmith, paranoid, werewolf"
- "Noble, charming, broke"
- "Beggar, wise, ex-king"

Then generate:

```
[ROLE] + [ADJECTIVE]  Hook (appearance + mannerism)
[SECRET]  Want (what are they hiding?) + Crack (why does it hurt?)
```

**Example**: "Blacksmith, paranoid, werewolf"

```
 MAGNUS IRONFOLD  The Twitchy Smith

Hook: Massive arms, bloodshot eyes. Flinches at loud
      noises. Won't work after sundown. Triple-locks
      his forge door with silver-inlaid bolts.

Want: Needs the party to retrieve Wolfsbane from the
      Thornwood  won't say why. Offers to forge
      anything they want for free. (Desperate.)

Crack: Lost his apprentice on the last full moon. Can't
       remember what happened. Found blood under his
       nails. Too afraid to find out the truth.
```

## Output Format

### Standard NPC Card (Tier 3)

```

               [NPC NAME]  [Epithet]


 HOOK
   [First impression: appearance + one mannerism + vibe]

 WANT
   Surface: [What they say they want]
   Truth:   [What they actually want]
   Line:    [One thing they'd never do]

 CRACK
   Flaw: [Their vulnerability]
   Show: [How the flaw manifests visibly]
   Bond: [One person who matters]

 DM NOTE
   [One sentence: how this NPC can connect to the plot]


```

### Minimal NPC Card (Tier 2)

```
 [NAME]  [Role]
   Look: [One visual detail]
   Want: [One sentence]
   Quirk: [One memorable trait]
```

## Examples

### Tier 3 Example: Quest Giver

```

         SISTER VELARA  The Smiling Warden


 HOOK
   Tall, gaunt woman in pristine white robes that
   never seem to stain. Smiles too much. Hands
   always clasped. Speaks like she's comforting
   a dying patient  even when discussing dinner.

 WANT
   Surface: Needs the party to clear undead from
            the old cemetery.
   Truth:   She created the undead by accident
            with a forbidden ritual. Needs the
            evidence destroyed before the order
            investigates.
   Line:    Won't hurt the living. Even to save
            herself. (Probably.)

 CRACK
   Flaw: Pathological need to be seen as good.
         Cannot admit fault  physically cannot.
   Show: Over-explains every decision. Volunteers
         for everything. Gives gifts unprompted.
   Bond: Brother Aldric  the only person who
         knows about the ritual. He's blackmailing
         her. She still loves him.

 DM NOTE
   If the party discovers the truth, Velara becomes
   either a powerful guilty ally or a desperate enemy.


```

### Tier 2 Example: Shopkeeper

```
 DORGO  Potion Seller
   Look: Missing two fingers on left hand (potion accident)
   Want: Sell the expired potions before anyone notices
   Quirk: Tastes every potion in front of the customer to "prove quality"
```

### Tier 2 Example: Guard

```
 CAPTAIN RENNA  City Gate Guard
   Look: Scar across nose, armor polished to mirror shine
   Want: Get through her shift without paperwork
   Quirk: Calls everyone "citizen" regardless of origin
```
