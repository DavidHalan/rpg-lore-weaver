# Party Creation Mode

A guide for creating multiple characters as an interconnected group. Instead of building each character in isolation, this mode weaves relationships, tensions, and shared history into the party from day one.

## When to Use

- The group is starting a new campaign and creating characters together
- A DM wants to pre-build a party of NPCs with rich internal dynamics
- Players want their characters to have shared history from session zero
- The group wants to ensure mechanical AND narrative coverage

---

## The Process

```
  Individual Creation     Party Creation Mode
  ──────────────────     ──────────────────────
  Player 1  Character   Step 1: Party Shape
  Player 2  Character   Step 2: Shared History
  Player 3  Character   Step 3: Relationship Web
  (no connections)        Step 4: Individual Creation
                           (with context from Steps 1-3)
                          Step 5: Party Dynamics Summary
```

---

## Step 1: Party Shape

Before any individual character is created, establish the group's identity:

**Ask the group:**

1. "How did you all meet? Choose one:"
   - A) We've known each other for years (existing bonds)
   - B) Thrown together by circumstance (strangers with a shared problem)
   - C) Mixed some know each other, some are new (subgroups within the party)

2. "What holds the group together?"
   - A) A shared mission or cause
   - B) A shared enemy or threat
   - C) Mutual dependency (each has something the others need)
   - D) Loyalty to a specific person or organization

3. "What's the group's biggest internal tension?"
   - Suggest options based on character concepts (see Tension Archetypes below)

---

## Step 2: Shared History Seeds

Before individual creation, plant 2-3 shared history elements that at least two characters will reference:

| Seed Type              | Example                                                                   |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Shared Event**       | "Two of you survived the same disaster. You've never talked about it."    |
| **Mutual Connection**  | "You both knew the same person but knew very different versions of them." |
| **Debt**               | "One of you saved another's life. It was never acknowledged."             |
| **Secret**             | "Two of you know something about each other that no one else does."       |
| **Shared Place**       | "You both consider the same place 'home' but for very different reasons." |
| **Opposing Loyalties** | "You serve the same cause but for incompatible reasons."                  |

**Rule**: Each character should connect to at least one shared history seed. No character exists in a vacuum.

---

## Step 3: Relationship Web

Before individual creation, establish the relationships between characters. For each pair, define:

```
  [Character A]  [Character B]
  Type: [Trust / Tension / Debt / Rivalry / Protection / Unknowing]
  Dynamic: [One sentence describing the relationship]
  Secret: [Something one knows about the other  or that neither knows yet]
```

### Relationship Starters (Roll d6 for each pair)

| Roll | Relationship                                                                               |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1    | A trusts B completely. B doesn't trust A at all.                                           |
| 2    | They were rivals before the party formed. The rivalry simmers.                             |
| 3    | One owes the other a favor. Neither agrees on what kind.                                   |
| 4    | They have complementary skills and mutual respect, but zero emotional connection.          |
| 5    | One reminds the other of someone they lost. They haven't said so.                          |
| 6    | They disagree on something fundamental (morality, strategy, faith) but respect each other. |

## Step 4: Individual Creation with Context

Now run each character through the standard 6-phase process, but with these modifications:

### Phase 1 (The Soil) additions:

- "How does your origin connect to the shared history seeds?"
- "Did you know any party members before the campaign started?"

### Phase 2 (The Engine) additions:

- "Does your motivation align or conflict with anyone else's?"
- "Would you ever prioritize a party member over your personal goal?"

### Phase 3 (The Cracks) additions:

- "Which party member sees your weakness most clearly?"
- "Whose weakness worries you the most?"

### Phase 4 (The Web) additions:

- "Among the party, who is your ally, your tension, and your mirror?"
- Ally = the one you'd protect first
- Tension = the one you clash with
- Mirror = the one who is too similar to you (which is its own problem)

### Phase 5 (The Synthesis) additions:

- Include a "Party Role" section in the character document
- Define what the character BRINGS to the group and what they TAKE from it

---

## Step 5: Party Dynamics Summary

After all characters are complete, generate a group summary:

```

   PARTY DYNAMICS  [Campaign Name]


   PARTY COMPOSITION
     [Character 1]  [One-line role]
     [Character 2]  [One-line role]
     [Character 3]  [One-line role]
     [Character 4]  [One-line role]

   STRONGEST BOND
     [Character A] ↔ [Character B]: [Why]

   MOST VOLATILE PAIR
     [Character C] ↔ [Character D]: [Why]

   SHARED VULNERABILITIES
     • [Something that threatens the whole group]
     • [An internal pressure point]

   PARTY-LEVEL DM HOOKS
     • [A hook that targets the group dynamic, not individuals]
     • [A situation that forces a choice between loyalty and mission]


```

## Tension Archetypes

Pre-built tension patterns for parties. Choose 1-2 per group:

| Tension                            | Description                                             | Example                                                     |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **The Idealist vs The Pragmatist** | One does what's right, one does what works              | Paladin vs Rogue archetype                                  |
| **The Leader Conflict**            | Two natural leaders with different styles               | Tactical mind vs Charismatic heart                          |
| **The Secret Keeper**              | One member hides something that would change everything | Hidden allegiance, past crime, true identity                |
| **The Moral Line**                 | The party agrees on the goal but not the methods        | "Do we torture the prisoner for information?"               |
| **The Outsider**                   | One member isn't fully accepted yet                     | New recruit, former enemy, different species/culture        |
| **The Protector Paradox**          | One protects another who doesn't want protecting        | Veteran shielding the youngest member                       |
| **The Mirror**                     | Two members are too similar, creating friction          | Both want to lead, both are loners, both have the same flaw |
| **The Ticking Clock**              | One member has a deadline the others may not know about | Curse, debt, prophecy, terminal condition                   |
