# Creative Decision Log

A running record of "why we chose X over Y" for each character element. Inspired by the decision log concept from structured brainstorming — applied to narrative design.

## Purpose

During character creation, many small decisions shape the final result. Without a log, the player forgets _why_ they chose certain elements — and the DM loses valuable insight into the player's creative intent.

The Creative Decision Log captures:

- What was decided
- What alternatives were considered
- Why this option was chosen
- What it means for the character

---

## When to Use

Maintain the log throughout the 6-phase creation process. Log a decision whenever:

- The player chooses between multiple options
- The AI offers A/B/C choices and one is selected
- A random table result is accepted, modified, or rejected
- An archetype is chosen or combined
- A contradiction or flaw is selected
- A relationship dynamic is defined

**Don't log everything** — focus on decisions that shaped the character's identity, not minor details.

---

## Log Format

```
══════════════════════════════════════════════════
  📝 CREATIVE DECISION LOG — [Character Name]
══════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Phase [#]: [Phase Name]
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────

  Decision: [What was decided]
  Alternatives: [What else was considered]
  Reason: [Why this option was chosen]
  Impact: [How this shapes the character]

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────

  [Repeat for each significant decision]

══════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

## Example Log

```
══════════════════════════════════════════════════
  📝 CREATIVE DECISION LOG — Kael Thornwood
══════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Phase 0: Discovery
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────

  Decision: Tiefling Druid (D&D 5e)
  Alternatives: Half-elf Ranger, Human Warlock
  Reason: Player wanted the tension between
          infernal heritage and nature devotion
  Impact: Core contradiction established early

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────

  Phase 1: The Soil
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────

  Decision: Raised by adoptive wood elf community
  Alternatives: (A) Orphanage in a city,
                (B) Raised by birth parents who
                    feared him, (C) Adopted by
                    wood elves who saw past the
                    horns
  Reason: Option C creates the richest tension —
          accepted by a community that values
          nature, but carrying infernal blood
  Impact: Establishes "outsider accepted but
          never quite belonging" as core theme

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────

  Phase 2: The Engine
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────

  Decision: Motivated by proving nature > blood
  Alternatives: Revenge against birth parents,
                generic "protect the forest"
  Reason: Player resonated with the idea that
          nurture defeats nature — personal
          connection
  Impact: Motivation is deeply tied to identity,
          not external events

  ─────────────────────────────────────────────

  Phase 3: The Cracks
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────

  Decision: Secret fear that infernal traits will
            emerge under stress
  Alternatives: Fear of rejection, fear of fire
  Reason: More dramatic — the thing he's trying
          to disprove IS the thing he fears most
  Impact: Creates a ticking clock for the DM —
          what happens when he loses control?

══════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

## Using the Log After Creation

### For the Player

- Review the log before sessions to remember creative intent
- When role-playing tough decisions, check: "What would my character do based on WHY I built them this way?"
- Share with the DM to help them create personalized challenges

### For the DM

- Use the "Alternatives" column as potential plot twists — what if the rejected option turns out to be true?
- The "Reason" column reveals what themes the player cares about — lean into those
- The "Impact" entries are pre-built story hooks

### For Character Evolution

- When updating the character after sessions (see `session-evolution.md`), add new entries to the Decision Log
- Track how in-play decisions differ from creation decisions — that's character growth
