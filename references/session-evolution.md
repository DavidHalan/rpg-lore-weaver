# Session Evolution Guide

How to update and evolve characters across multiple sessions. The initial creation is just the beginning  characters grow, change, and break in play.

## When to Use

Use this guide when a player says any of the following:

- "My character would be different after what happened last session"
- "I want to update my backstory"
- "How would this event change my character?"
- "My character has been through a lot  I need to reflect that"

Or proactively after any of these events:

- A major NPC was killed, betrayed, or revealed
- The character experienced a significant failure or victory
- A moral line was crossed (or held)
- A secret was revealed
- The character's beliefs were directly challenged

---

## Post-Session Debrief Questions

Ask the player **3-5 of these** after significant sessions:

### Emotional Check

1. "What is your character feeling RIGHT NOW, after what happened?"
2. "Is there something they wish they had done differently?"
3. "Who did they think about when things got dangerous?"

### Relationship Shifts

4. "Has your opinion of any party member changed? Why?"
5. "Is there someone new your character trusts more? Or less?"
6. "Did an NPC surprise your character? How?"

### Belief Stress Test

7. "Did anything challenge what your character believes in?"
8. "Is there a rule or principle they bent or broke tonight?"
9. "What would past-them think of what they just did?"

### Growth Signals

10. "Did your character learn something they can't unlearn?"
11. "What small habit or quirk might change after this?"
12. "Is the 'Elevator Pitch' still accurate, or does it need updating?"

## The Evolution Tracker

Add this section to the character document after each significant arc:

```

   EVOLUTION LOG


  Session/Arc: [Name or number]
  Date: [Real-world date]

   WHAT CHANGED
  • [Pillar affected]: [Brief description of change]
  • [Pillar affected]: [Brief description of change]

   CRACKS THAT DEEPENED
  • [Existing flaw/weakness that got worse or was tested]

   GROWTH
  • [New skill, relationship, or understanding gained]

   UPDATED ELEVATOR PITCH
  "[Revised one-sentence summary if needed]"

   NEW HOOKS GENERATED
  • [Any new loose threads from this session]


```

## Pillar Review Checklist

After a major arc, review each pillar and note what changed:

| Pillar        | Question                                                           | Update Needed? |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------- |
| Origin        | Has new information about their past been revealed?                |                |
| Family        | Have family relationships shifted (reunion, loss, betrayal)?       |                |
| Motivations   | Is the original motivation still the primary driver?               |                |
| Personality   | Have they become harder, softer, more guarded, more open?          |                |
| Ideals        | Were any ideals tested or broken?                                  |                |
| Weaknesses    | Did they overcome a weakness or gain a new one?                    |                |
| Key Decisions | What was the biggest decision this arc? Will it haunt them?        |                |
| Friends       | Did they gain or lose an ally?                                     |                |
| Mentors       | Did the mentor relationship evolve (surpassed, lost, complicated)? |                |
| Rivals        | Did the rival arc resolve, escalate, or transform?                 |                |

## Character Arc Patterns

Common patterns for character evolution over a campaign:

### Flashback Moments (Intra-Session Depth)

Don't wait for the backstory to be revealed linearly. Use **Flashbacks** during short rests or travel.

**The Prompt**: _"While you stare into the campfire, a memory surfaces. Not a big one, just a fragment. What do you see?"_

**Triggers for Flashbacks**:

- Hearing a specific song
- The smell of a certain food
- Examining a scar
- A quiet moment of safety after danger

> **Goal**: Reveal _emotion_, not just facts. We know your village burned. We don't know that you still smell the smoke every time you toast bread.

---

### The Descent

Character starts strong and is gradually broken by the world. Each session peels away another layer of confidence or belief.

- **Early sessions**: Competent, idealistic
- **Mid-campaign**: Doubting, compromising
- **Late campaign**: Either breaks completely or finds a bedrock truth to stand on

### The Redemption

Character starts in a dark place and slowly earns their way back through choices.

- **Early sessions**: Distrusted, carrying guilt
- **Mid-campaign**: Proving themselves through action, not words
- **Late campaign**: A defining moment where they choose right at great personal cost

### The Revelation

Character's true nature or history is gradually revealed, changing how everyone sees them.

- **Early sessions**: Hiding something fundamental
- **Mid-campaign**: Cracks in the facade, close calls
- **Late campaign**: Full revelation and the consequences that follow

### The Transformation

Character becomes something fundamentally different from what they started as.

- **Early sessions**: Comfortable in their role
- **Mid-campaign**: The role stops fitting  new powers, new priorities, new worldview
- **Late campaign**: Emergence as a new version of themselves, for better or worse

### The Anchor

Character remains consistent while the world changes around them. Their stability is both strength and limitation.

- **Early sessions**: Reliable, principled
- **Mid-campaign**: Their consistency is tested as allies change
- **Late campaign**: Either proves that some things shouldn't change, or discovers they've been standing still while everyone else grew

## Adding New Sections to the Character Document

As the character evolves, consider adding these optional sections:

### Scars (Physical & Emotional)

A running list of wounds that left a mark:

```
 SCARS
  • [Physical]: [What happened, when, visible?]
  • [Emotional]: [What happened, how it manifests]
```

### Regrets

Decisions that haunt:

```
 REGRETS
  • [Decision]: [Why it haunts them, would they do it differently?]
```

### New Bonds

Relationships formed during play:

```
 BONDS (In-Play)
  • [Name]: [How they met, what they mean now]
```

### Reputation

How the world sees them vs how they see themselves:

```
 REPUTATION
  • Allies see: [Perception]
  • Enemies see: [Perception]
  • Strangers see: [Perception]
  • They see themselves as: [Self-perception]
```
