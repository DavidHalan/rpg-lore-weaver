## How to Use

1. When a player is stuck on a specific pillar, roll on the relevant table
2. Treat results as **inspiration, not obligation** — the player can twist, modify, or reject
3. Combine results from multiple tables for complex characters
4. Works with any RPG system — adapt terminology as needed

## Origin Sparks (d10)

_"Where did you come from?"_ — Use when Phase 1 stalls.

| Roll | Origin                                                                                                             |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1    | Raised by a **single parent** who disappeared when you were a teenager. You never learned why.                     |
| 2    | Grew up in a **traveling caravan/troupe** — you've never stayed anywhere longer than a season.                     |
| 3    | Orphan raised by an **institution** (temple, military, organization). Love was structured, never spontaneous.      |
| 4    | Your family was **wealthy but emotionally cold**. You had everything except warmth.                                |
| 5    | Grew up on the **frontier/border** between two cultures. You belong to both and neither.                           |
| 6    | Raised by a **non-biological family** who took you in after a disaster. You carry the gratitude like a debt.       |
| 7    | Your hometown was **destroyed** when you were young. You rebuilt a life somewhere else, but the accent never left. |
| 8    | You were the **middle child** in a large family — invisible between the achiever and the troublemaker.             |
| 9    | Grew up in **isolated conditions** (island, mountain, underground). The wider world still overwhelms you.          |
| 10   | You were **secretly adopted** and only recently discovered your birth family exists.                               |

## Childhood Trauma (d8)

_"What broke you early?"_ — Use when Phase 3 needs a deeper root.

| Roll | Trauma                                                                                                       |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1    | **Witnessed something** you were too young to understand. You understand it now. You wish you didn't.        |
| 2    | **Betrayed by a trusted figure** — a teacher, a parent, a mentor. Trust has been currency ever since.        |
| 3    | **Responsible for an accident** that hurt someone. You were a child. You still blame yourself.               |
| 4    | **Publicly humiliated** in a way that shaped your entire personality. You've built walls or weapons since.   |
| 5    | **Lost someone** to a preventable cause. You've dedicated your life to preventing it from happening again.   |
| 6    | **Forced to grow up too fast** — became the parent, the provider, the responsible one before you were ready. |
| 7    | **Discovered a family secret** that changed how you see everyone who raised you.                             |
| 8    | **Survived something** that no one believes actually happened. You stopped talking about it years ago.       |

## Secret Motivation (d10)

_"What REALLY drives you — the thing you don't say out loud?"_ — Use when Phase 2 feels surface-level.

| Roll | Motivation                                                                                                                |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | You're **running from someone** — and the adventure is an elaborate way to keep moving.                                   |
| 2    | You need **money** desperately. Not for greed — for a debt, a ransom, a treatment, a family obligation.                   |
| 3    | You're **searching for someone** who disappeared. Every new place is a chance to find a trace.                            |
| 4    | You're trying to **prove someone wrong** — a parent, a teacher, a rival who said you'd never amount to anything.          |
| 5    | You're **atoning** for something the rest of the group doesn't know about.                                                |
| 6    | You **crave recognition** because you were invisible your whole life. The mission is a stage.                             |
| 7    | You're collecting **information** for a third party. You're not a spy — but what's the difference, really?                |
| 8    | You didn't **choose** this life. You were coerced, blackmailed, or had no alternative. You stay because leaving is worse. |
| 9    | You want to **discover the truth** about an event, a mystery, or a phenomenon — even if the truth is dangerous.           |
| 10   | You're looking for a **reason to keep going**. Not suicidal — just profoundly lost. The mission gives structure.          |

## Signature Quirk (d12)

_"What small thing makes them unforgettable?"_ — Use to add texture to any phase.

| Roll | Quirk                                                                                                          |
| ---- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | Always carries **a specific object** (a coin, a letter, a stone) and touches it when thinking.                 |
| 2    | **Hums or whistles** the same melody — can't remember where they learned it.                                   |
| 3    | **Can't sit still** — always pacing, fidgeting, tapping. Silence is their enemy.                               |
| 4    | **Writes everything down** in a journal. Won't let anyone read it. Gets anxious if it's out of reach.          |
| 5    | **Apologizes too much** — for things that aren't their fault, for existing in a space, for speaking.           |
| 6    | Has an unusual **dietary restriction** with a story behind it. (Vegetarian barbarian. Teetotal pirate.)        |
| 7    | **Counts things** — doors, steps, exits. Not OCD, just survival instinct baked into habit.                     |
| 8    | Gives **nicknames** to everyone and everything. Rarely uses real names.                                        |
| 9    | Always **lies about small things** (age, hometown, favorite color) but is brutally honest about big ones.      |
| 10   | **Sleeps badly** — light sleeper, nightmares, or simply can't function before dawn.                            |
| 11   | Has a **physical tell** when lying — touches their ear, looks left, smiles too wide.                           |
| 12   | **Collects something** odd (bottle caps, feathers, interesting rocks). The collection has an unspoken pattern. |

## Hidden Connection (d8)

_"How is this NPC secretly tied to you?"_ — Use to generate surprise links in Phase 4.

| Roll | Connection                                                                                                   |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1    | They knew your **parent/mentor** and have opinions about you based on that person.                           |
| 2    | You **owe them** something — or they owe you — from a shared event neither of you talks about.               |
| 3    | They are a **relative** you didn't know existed. The resemblance is unsettling.                              |
| 4    | You were both **present at the same event** but experienced it from opposite sides.                          |
| 5    | They carry a **piece of information** about your past that could change everything — and they don't know it. |
| 6    | You were once **rivals** for the same opportunity. One of you won. Neither has forgotten.                    |
| 7    | They were **kind to you once** during a dark time. They don't remember. You'll never forget.                 |
| 8    | You've seen them **in a dream** or a vision. You recognize them. They've never seen you before.              |

## Internal Contradiction (d10)

_"What two truths about you can't coexist?"_ — Use when a character feels one-dimensional.

| Roll | Contradiction                                                                                                         |
| ---- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | **Healer who enjoys violence.** Helps people because it's right, but the combat rush is real.                         |
| 2    | **Leader who hates responsibility.** People follow them naturally, and it terrifies them.                             |
| 3    | **Pacifist with lethal skills.** Trained to kill, chose not to. But the training doesn't disappear.                   |
| 4    | **Liar who values truth.** Believes honesty is sacred, but lies effortlessly and frequently.                          |
| 5    | **Loner who craves connection.** Pushes people away while desperately wanting them to stay.                           |
| 6    | **Scholar who distrusts knowledge.** Reads everything, questions everything, trusts nothing they read.                |
| 7    | **Protector who is the danger.** Guards allies from threats while being the biggest threat in the room.               |
| 8    | **Optimist who expects the worst.** Plans for disaster with a smile. "Hope for the best, prepare for the apocalypse." |
| 9    | **Freedom-lover who follows orders.** Hates authority but obeys because discipline saved their life once.             |
| 10   | **Kind person who refuses mercy.** Gentle with friends, absolutely ruthless with enemies. No middle ground.           |

## DM Hook Seeds (d10)

_"What thread is dangling from this character?"_ — Use in Phase 5 or NPC Quick Mode.

| Roll | Hook                                                                                                             |
| ---- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | A **sealed letter** they were told to open only "when everything falls apart." They haven't opened it yet.       |
| 2    | They keep **dreaming about a place** they've never been. The dreams are getting more detailed.                   |
| 3    | Someone is **leaving coded messages** for them in public places. They haven't responded yet.                     |
| 4    | An old **debt collector** has started sending representatives. The debt isn't money — it's a favor owed.         |
| 5    | Their dead mentor's **journal** contains a final entry that doesn't make sense unless the mentor is still alive. |
| 6    | A **stranger recognized them** in a city they've never visited and called them by a different name.              |
| 7    | They found a **map fragment** among a relative's belongings. It shows a location that shouldn't exist.           |
| 8    | A **childhood friend** has resurfaced — changed, powerful, and asking for help with something uncomfortable.     |
| 9    | They've discovered that a **decision they made years ago** had consequences they're only now learning about.     |
| 10   | Something they **killed/destroyed/sealed** is showing signs of coming back.                                      |

## NPC Relationship Twists (d8)

_"What makes this relationship not what it seems?"_ — Use with NPC Quick Mode or Phase 4.

| Roll | Twist                                                                                                                            |
| ---- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | The **ally** is reporting everything to a third party. Not maliciously — they genuinely think it's for the best.                 |
| 2    | The **mentor** lied about something foundational. The lesson was real, but the reason behind it was a cover.                     |
| 3    | The **rival** actually admires the character. The rivalry is how they express respect.                                           |
| 4    | The **ally** is dying and hasn't told anyone. Their urgency about the mission isn't passion — it's deadline.                     |
| 5    | The **rival** and the character share an enemy. They should be allies but can't get past their history.                          |
| 6    | The **mentor** is testing the character — the current crisis is engineered. The real test is how they handle it.                 |
| 7    | The **ally** has powers or resources they've been hiding. They're afraid of how the character would react.                       |
| 8    | The **rival** was once the mentor's previous student. They failed where the character is succeeding. The resentment is personal. |
