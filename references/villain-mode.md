# Villain / Antagonist Creation Mode

> Activated when the user selects **🗡️ Villain / Antagonist** in Discovery Step 0.

This workflow creates memorable, three-dimensional antagonists with clear motivations, concrete plans, and exploitable vulnerabilities. A great villain is not the opposite of the hero — they are a dark mirror.

---

## Core Philosophy

> "Every villain is a hero of their own story."

A compelling antagonist needs:

1. **A reason the audience almost agrees with** — their logic should be seductive
2. **A line they crossed** — the moment they chose wrong
3. **A weakness the PCs can discover** — not a flaw, but a _crack_

---

## The Villain Workflow

### V1: The Seed

**Ask the user:**

- What role does this villain play? (BBEG, recurring rival, corrupted ally, faction leader, force of nature)
- What system and setting? (Apply `systems/` module if available)
- What is the tone? (Tragic, terrifying, charismatic, bureaucratic, monstrous)
- How personal is the conflict with the PCs?

### V2: The Mirror (Motivation)

Build the villain's motivation using this framework:

- **The Wound**: What happened to them that broke the world's promise?
- **The Logic**: Why does their solution _make sense_ (to them)?
- **The Line**: What was the first thing they did that they can never take back?
- **Push vs Pull**: Are they driven _away from_ pain or _toward_ a vision?

> **Anti-pattern warning**: "They're just evil" is not a motivation. Even a demonic entity should have a _preference_, a _hunger_, a _grudge_.

### V3: The Plan

Every villain needs a concrete objective:

- **Goal**: What do they want? (Power, revenge, salvation, order, freedom, knowledge)
- **Method**: How are they achieving it right now? (What are the PCs interrupting?)
- **Timeline**: What happens if nobody stops them? (Escalation beats)
- **Resources**: Who works for them and why? (Minions with their own motivations)

### V4: The Cracks (Vulnerability & Humanity)

What makes this villain beatable — and what makes them _tragic_:

- **Blind Spot**: What can't they see about themselves?
- **The One Thing They Protect**: Who or what do they still care about?
- **The Tell**: A behavioral pattern that reveals their true state (nervous tic, obsessive ritual, who they won't hurt)
- **Redemption Potential**: On a scale of 1-5, how salvageable are they? (This shapes the narrative endgame)

### V5: The Web (Relationship to PCs)

The villain must connect to the party — not just oppose them:

- **Why these PCs?** Why is this villain _their_ problem specifically?
- **Shared History**: Is there a past connection (mentor, family, same village, same war)?
- **What they offer**: What temptation does the villain represent for each PC?
- **Respect or Contempt**: Does the villain see the PCs as threats, tools, or insects?

### V6: Escalation Arc

How the villain's threat grows across the campaign:

| Stage             | Description                                                  |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Introduction**  | Consequences of villain's actions visible but villain unseen |
| **Revelation**    | Villain revealed — their logic is almost convincing          |
| **Confrontation** | First direct encounter — villain demonstrates superiority    |
| **Retaliation**   | Villain responds to PCs' interference — it gets personal     |
| **Climax**        | Final confrontation — PCs must exploit the crack             |

## Tips for Villain Creation

- **Mirror a PC**: The best villains share a goal with a PC but took a different path
- **Show, don't tell**: Introduce the villain through consequences before appearance
- **Give them wins**: A villain who always loses is not threatening
- **Let them be right about something**: Partial truth is more dangerous than lies
- **Minions reflect the master**: A villain's followers reveal their values
