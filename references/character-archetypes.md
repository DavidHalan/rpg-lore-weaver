# Character Archetypes

A bank of universal narrative archetypes to inspire character creation. These transcend any specific RPG system — they are storytelling patterns that work in fantasy, sci-fi, horror, or any genre.

## Table of Contents

- [How to Use](#how-to-use)
- [The 20 Archetypes](#the-20-archetypes)
- [Combining Archetypes](#combining-archetypes)

---

## How to Use

When a player is stuck or says "I don't know what kind of character I want," offer 3-4 archetypes from this list as starting points. The player picks one that resonates, and you use it as a **narrative skeleton** — then fill in the bones with the 10 pillars.

**Important**: Archetypes are starting points, not cages. The best characters **subvert** their archetype by the end of the story.

---

## The 20 Archetypes

### 1. The Reluctant Hero

> "I never asked for this."

Someone chosen by circumstance, not ambition. They have the skill but lack the desire. Their arc is about accepting responsibility.

- **Strength**: Competence under pressure
- **Crack**: Avoidance, self-doubt, resentment toward the role
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS to go home. NEEDS to accept they've already changed too much to go back.
- **Systems**: Works everywhere. Especially strong in D&D (farmer-turned-adventurer), Ordem Paranormal (reluctant agent).

---

### 2. The Fallen Noble

> "I had everything. Then I had nothing. Now I have a purpose."

Once privileged, now stripped of status. Their arc is about discovering identity beyond title.

- **Strength**: Education, etiquette, hidden resources
- **Crack**: Pride, inability to ask for help, lingering entitlement
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS to reclaim their former position. NEEDS to realize they're better without it.
- **Systems**: D&D (exiled prince), Tormenta 20 (displaced from a kingdom destroyed by the Tormenta).

---

### 3. The Atonement Seeker

> "I can't undo what I did. But I can die trying."

Haunted by a past crime or failure. Everything they do is an attempt to balance the scales.

- **Strength**: Unshakeable resolve, willingness to sacrifice
- **Crack**: Self-destructive tendencies, inability to forgive themselves, martyrdom complex
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS forgiveness from others. NEEDS to forgive themselves.
- **Systems**: Pathfinder (fallen paladin), Call of Cthulhu (investigator who got someone killed), Daggerheart.

---

### 4. The Masked Optimist

> "Everything is fine." (Everything is not fine.)

Projects cheerfulness to hide deep pain. The group's emotional anchor who is secretly falling apart.

- **Strength**: Morale, emotional support for others, resilience facade
- **Crack**: Bottled emotions, eventual breakdown, inability to accept help
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS everyone else to be okay. NEEDS to admit they're not okay either.
- **Systems**: Daggerheart (Hope/Fear tension), D&D (bard hiding grief).

---

### 5. The Outsider Trying to Belong

> "Am I one of you yet?"

Rejected by origin or nature. Constantly performing normalcy.

- **Strength**: Adaptability, keen observation (you watch people when you're not like them)
- **Crack**: Imposter syndrome, over-compensation, explosive anger when rejected
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS acceptance from the group. NEEDS to accept themselves first.
- **Systems**: D&D (tiefling, half-orc), Tormenta 20 (Lefou), Pathfinder (uncommon ancestry).

---

### 6. The Knowledge Addict

> "I need to know. Even if it kills me."

Driven by curiosity past all sane limits. The line between scholar and madman is thin.

- **Strength**: Intelligence, problem-solving, encyclopedic knowledge
- **Crack**: Obsession that overrides safety, self, and relationships
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS answers. NEEDS to learn that some questions are the answer.
- **Systems**: Call of Cthulhu (the classic), Ordem Paranormal (agents drawn to O Outro Lado).

---

### 7. The Broken Protector

> "I couldn't save them. I'll save everyone else."

Failed to protect someone important. Now protects compulsively — even at their own expense.

- **Strength**: Unwavering loyalty, combat skill born from desperation
- **Crack**: Overprotectiveness that suffocates others, inability to let people take risks
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS to never lose anyone again. NEEDS to accept that protection isn't control.
- **Systems**: Universal. Works in every system and genre.

---

### 8. The Con Artist with a Heart

> "I lie for a living. But I'll never lie to you."

Deception is their tool, but somewhere underneath is genuine loyalty.

- **Strength**: Charisma, improvisation, reading people
- **Crack**: Nobody trusts them (for good reason), the lies eventually catch up
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS to pull off the big score. NEEDS to find something worth being honest for.
- **Systems**: D&D (rogue), Pathfinder (charlatan background).

---

### 9. The Corrupted Idealist

> "The world made me this way."

Started with pure ideals. Reality broke them. Now they use morally grey methods for originally noble goals.

- **Strength**: Strategic thinking, willingness to do what others won't
- **Crack**: Sliding moral compass, alienating allies, becoming what they fought against
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS to achieve their original goal by any means. NEEDS to remember why the goal mattered in the first place.
- **Systems**: Ordem Paranormal (agent who's seen too much), Tormenta 20 (disillusioned servant of a Divindade).

---

### 10. The Living Weapon

> "I was built for war. Is that all I am?"

Trained, altered, or created for violence. Searching for meaning beyond their function.

- **Strength**: Combat mastery, discipline, physical prowess
- **Crack**: Emotional repression, identity crisis, fear of peace
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS a purpose beyond fighting. NEEDS to discover they already have one.
- **Systems**: D&D (warforged, soldier background), Tormenta 20 (Golem).

---

### 11. The Reluctant Heir

> "This was never my destiny. It was theirs."

Inheriting a role, title, or burden meant for someone else — someone who died, disappeared, or refused.

- **Strength**: Resources and connections they didn't earn, fresh perspective on old problems
- **Crack**: Imposter syndrome, resentment, living in a shadow
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS to prove they deserve the role. NEEDS to stop measuring themselves against the original.
- **Systems**: Pathfinder (noble background), D&D (warlock whose pact was inherited).

---

### 12. The Monster Within

> "The most dangerous thing in this room is me."

Carries something inside them — a curse, a power, a second nature — that threatens to consume them.

- **Strength**: Raw power when they let go, unique perspective on darkness
- **Crack**: Fear of self, isolation, harm to loved ones during loss of control
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS to cure or remove the condition. NEEDS to integrate it as part of who they are.
- **Systems**: Tormenta 20 (Lefou with Tormenta corruption), Ordem Paranormal (high NEX agent), Daggerheart.

---

### 13. The True Believer

> "My faith is the only certainty in an uncertain world."

Unshakeable devotion to a cause, deity, or philosophy — until the cracks appear.

- **Strength**: Conviction, resilience, community support
- **Crack**: Rigidity, crisis of faith when doctrine meets reality, intolerance
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS the world to match their belief. NEEDS to accept that faith is strongest when it survives doubt.
- **Systems**: D&D (cleric/paladin), Tormenta 20 (devoto), Pathfinder (champion).

---

### 14. The Debt Carrier

> "I owe everything to someone. Even if they're gone."

Lives under the weight of a debt — financial, moral, or existential. Everything is tinted by obligation.

- **Strength**: Reliability, determination, sense of duty
- **Crack**: Self-sacrifice disguised as honor, inability to live for themselves
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS to repay the debt. NEEDS to realize they've been paying it all along.
- **Systems**: Universal. Strong in systems with patron mechanics (D&D warlock, Pathfinder oracle).

---

### 15. The Last Survivor

> "Everyone I knew is gone. I'm still here. I don't know why."

Sole survivor of a catastrophe. Carries grief and guilt in equal measure.

- **Strength**: Resilience, survival instincts, appreciation for small things
- **Crack**: Survivor's guilt, emotional numbness, recklessness born from "nothing left to lose"
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS to find meaning in their survival. NEEDS to stop surviving and start living.
- **Systems**: Call of Cthulhu, Tormenta 20 (Tormenta survivor), Ordem Paranormal.

---

### 16. The Double Life

> "They don't know who I really am. Neither do I, anymore."

Maintains two identities. The tension between them is the story.

- **Strength**: Adaptability, compartmentalization, two sets of skills
- **Crack**: Exhaustion, paranoia, the inevitable collision of both lives
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS to keep both lives intact. NEEDS to choose — or find a self that doesn't require masks.
- **Systems**: Ordem Paranormal (agent hiding from family), D&D (spy/infiltrator).

---

### 17. The Mentor's Shadow

> "They taught me everything. Except how to be myself."

Shaped entirely by a powerful mentor. Now struggling to find their own voice.

- **Strength**: Well-trained, disciplined, loyal
- **Crack**: Lack of identity independent of the mentor, difficulty making original decisions
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS to make their mentor proud. NEEDS to make themselves proud.
- **Systems**: Pathfinder, D&D, Daggerheart.

---

### 18. The Wanderer with a Map

> "I'm not lost. I just haven't found it yet."

Searching for something specific — a place, a person, an object, a feeling — across the world.

- **Strength**: Resourcefulness, diverse knowledge from travels, independence
- **Crack**: Inability to commit, using the quest as an excuse to avoid settling
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS to find the thing they're searching for. NEEDS to realize the search itself is what gives their life meaning.
- **Systems**: Universal. All systems support travelers and seekers.

---

### 19. The Quiet Authority

> "I don't raise my voice. I don't need to."

Leads through competence and presence, not volume. Calm until they're terrifying.

- **Strength**: Natural leadership, emotional control, strategic mind
- **Crack**: Emotional repression, the weight of decisions, loneliness at the top
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS to keep everyone safe through control. NEEDS to trust others enough to share the burden.
- **Systems**: D&D (leader/commander), Ordem Paranormal (senior agent), Call of Cthulhu.

---

### 20. The Joyful Destroyer

> "Life is too short for regrets. Pass me the dynamite."

Chaotic, exuberant, and probably a liability. But also the most alive person in the room.

- **Strength**: Creativity, fearlessness, ability to break stalemates
- **Crack**: Recklessness, inability to take things seriously, hidden pain behind the humor
- **Classic Want vs Need**: WANTS excitement and freedom. NEEDS to find something worth being careful for.
- **Systems**: Tormenta 20 (follower of Nimb), D&D (wild magic sorcerer), Daggerheart.

---

## Combining Archetypes

The most compelling characters blend TWO archetypes that create internal tension:

| Combination                        | Tension                                                            |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Reluctant Hero + Knowledge Addict  | Doesn't want the quest, but can't stop investigating               |
| Fallen Noble + Con Artist          | Uses old etiquette skills for new cons — but misses the real thing |
| Broken Protector + Monster Within  | Wants to protect but IS the danger                                 |
| True Believer + Corrupted Idealist | Faith that's been twisted by experience                            |
| Masked Optimist + Last Survivor    | Smiles brightest because the alternative is collapse               |
| Double Life + Outsider             | Neither identity feels real                                        |

**How to use combinations**: Pick one archetype as the **surface** (what others see) and one as the **core** (what drives them underneath). The gap between surface and core is where drama lives.
